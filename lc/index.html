Everyone knows a real man doesn't rely on Leetcode to hone his skills. Traversing a binary tree and performing a merge sort come naturally to him. He was born with a gift, and everybody can sense it, particularly women. He needn't expel his virtues through his nostrils lest his waifish neck collapse from the weight of his massive brain. Nor must he witness the dreaded eye roll, the furrowed brow, the half-quizzical, half-disgusted, half-open mouth of his inicipient sweetheart as she subtly distances herself upon learning he spent the weekend timing himself practicing Leetcode Hard. No, a real man would be ashamed to ever let anyone see him feverishly hitting submit for the 30th time at 2 a.m. on a Friday night praying to be Accepted. The real man has already been Accepted. He needn't the recognition of his lessers, and if he does, he has enough common sense to at least clear his browser history afterwards.

I, Dear Readers, am unfortunately not that man.
