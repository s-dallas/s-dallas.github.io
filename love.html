on love

What does it mean to love? To love someone deeply, to become so enamored that it becomes difficult to think about anything else? The loved one may no longer exist but still appears in one’s dreams. The memories persist and one feels compelled to chase after them, or the traces of what they seem to portend, even if it be in another, knowing that the pursuit is likely to culminate in an armful of vapors. To love is to be discontented. One may love the idea of love, the reflection one sees in the eyes of the beloved. One may love and be unloved. One may love and lose. One may have one’s heart tempered so that any mention of the term becomes verboten. One may find that ordering one’s life in pursuit of love is the only way to live, and that making oneself a fool again and again is less a Sisyphean undertaking and more akin to learning how to ride a bicycle. The sting of abraded knees and elbows cannot be felt through the wind. That momentary thrill, the reckless abandon, the forgetting of the self. We say it is the madman who makes this his life’s work, but part of us knows he is the only sane one among us. To love is to be contented in a world that operates on discontent. To love is to be alive and there are no apologies to be made for the living.
